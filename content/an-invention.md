The Zhang family always had a rule. After dinner, the family would flip a four-sided dice to decide who would do the dishes for the day. They would crowd around in a circle, waiting for the answer in restless anticipation. On this particular night, the dice said one.

"Just my lucky day," smiled the father Joel without any sarcasm, "Now you two can go do your homework earlier".

The two kids gave him a half smile and made their way back to their rooms. They thanked the gods for having escaped the burden of doing the chore. Such cumbersome work dishes are, how repetitive, how unromantic, how unfit for an easily distracted kid; made even worse by the meddling from their fathers.
