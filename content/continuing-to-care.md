I have been feeling a strange sense of disappointed confusion recently. Ever since this semester's classes ended, I have been wondering why I should care about my own life anymore. Not as some sort of self-pitying exercise, but rather to figure out what the point of trying so hard even is anymore.

I've lived a majority of my life basically waiting for it to start. How great would becoming free from parental control be? How great would it be once I looked better? How great would life be if I got my own money? These questions floated around in my head constantly, and they were a great coping mechanism through the harder points in my life. But as I moved back home for the summer, fully alone and without major responsibility for the first time in my life, I've realized that these soothing thoughts have completely lost their comforting effect. That helpless me has become all that I am, and those dreams became just as confused and powerless as I always felt over my own life.

In the place of those dreams remains my standards. Like lots of young people, I hold high standards for myself. Although something about that admission feels very embarrassing. It's a sort of display of weakness, isn't it? Implicit in the standard for onself is the message the one is not enough, that to feel secure some arbitrary change must be made, whether that be grades, looks, money, intelligence or whatever else. I like to think that getting closer to my goalposts would make me happier and more secure, it certainly has worked for me in the past, but nowadays I am not too sure. In fact, I find myself unable to come to terms with the fact that I am not an easy-going person, I am judgy at times, I feel insecure, and many of the traits that I find unpleasant in others have found its way into me.  The truth is, I am jealous of people who don't play the overachiever game anymore. The people who have the strength or privilege to not torture themselves.

These ideals are a blinding light on the top of an endless staircase, magnificant, formless and forever out of reach. The promise of reaching the top is so enticing that giving up on one feels so incredibly hard.
